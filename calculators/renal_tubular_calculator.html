Shared Laboratory Values

Fractional Excretion of Sodium (FENa)

FENa = (UNa × PCr) / (PNa × UCr) × 100
--
%

Interpretation

  • < 1%: Pre-renal azotemia (appropriate conservation)
  • > 1-2%: Intrinsic tubular injury or salt wasting
  • False Positives: Diuretics, CKD, metabolic alkalosis

Fractional Excretion of Chloride (FECl)

FECl = (UCl × PCr) / (PCl × UCr) × 100
--
%

Interpretation (Metabolic Alkalosis)

  • < 1%: True volume depletion (chloride-responsive)
  • > 1-2%: Renal chloride wasting (Bartter, Gitelman, diuretics)

Fractional Excretion of Urea (FEUrea)

FEUrea = (UUrea × PCr) / (PUrea × UCr) × 100
--
%

Interpretation (On Diuretics)

  • < 35%: Pre-renal state (volume depletion)
  • > 50-65%: Intrinsic tubular injury (ATN)
  • Advantage: Urea handling is proximal; less affected by diuretics

Fractional Excretion of Uric Acid (FEUA)

FEUA = (UUA × PCr) / (PUA × UCr) × 100
--
%

SIADH vs Salt Wasting (The Diagnostic Flip)

  • Normal: < 10%
  • After correcting hyponatremia: Normalizes in SIADH; remains >11% in Renal Salt Wasting

Transtubular Potassium Gradient (TTKG)

Prerequisite: UOsm > POsm and UNa > 25 mEq/L
TTKG = [UK / (UOsm / POsm)] / PK
--
unitless

Interpretation in Hypokalemia

  • TTKG > 4: Renal K wasting (inappropriate aldosterone activity or distal non-responsiveness)
  • TTKG < 3: Appropriate renal conservation (extra-renal loss)

Fractional Excretion of Potassium (FEK)

FEK = (UK × PCr) / (PK × UCr) × 100
--
%

Interpretation in Hypokalemia

  • > 6-10%: Inappropriate renal K wasting
  • Dependent on intake; less specific than TTKG

Urine K/Creatinine Ratio

Ratio = UK (mEq/L) / UCr (g/L)

1 g/L = 100 mg/dL. Select appropriately.

--
mEq/g

Interpretation in Hypokalemia

  • > 13 mEq/g (or > 1.5 mmol/mmol): Renal wasting
  • < 13 mEq/g: GI loss or intracellular shift

Urine Anion Gap (UAG)

UAG = UNa + UK - UCl
--
mEq/L

Interpretation (NAGMA)

  • Negative (-20 to -50): Kidney producing NH4+ appropriately. Acidosis likely extra-renal (diarrhea).
  • Positive: Failing to excrete NH4+. Distal (Type 1) or Hypoaldosterone (Type 4) RTA.

Urine Osmolar Gap (UOG)

UOG = Measured UOsm - Calculated UOsm
Calc UOsm = 2×(UNa+UK) + UBUN/2.8 + UGlucose/18
--
mOsm/kg

Interpretation (Robust NH4+ Surrogate)

  • Normal gap: 10-100 mOsm/kg
  • Metabolic acidosis + gap > 150: Appropriate renal response
  • Low UOG during acidosis: Intrinsic RTA (Type 1 or 2)

Fractional Excretion of Phosphorus (FEPO4)

FEPO4 = (UPO4 × PCr) / (PPO4 × UCr) × 100
--
%

Interpretation

  • Normal: 5-20%
  • Elevated: Proximal tubulopathy (Fanconi-like)

Tubular Reabsorption of Phosphate (TRP)

TRP = 1 - (UPO4 × PCr) / (PPO4 × UCr)
--
fraction

TmP/GFR (Walton & Bijvoet)

If TRP ≤ 0.86: TmP/GFR = PPO4 × TRP
If TRP > 0.86: TmP/GFR = PPO4 × (0.3 × TRP) / (1 - 0.8 × TRP)
--
mg/dL

Interpretation (GFR-Independent)

  • Low TmP/GFR: Confirms primary proximal tubular phosphate wasting (FGF23 excess, Fanconi syndrome)
  • Independent of patient's renal function

Fractional Excretion of Magnesium (FEMg)

FEMg = (UMg × PCr) / (0.7 × PMg × UCr) × 100

0.7 factor because only ~70% of plasma Mg is ultrafiltrable at the glomerulus.

--
%

Interpretation in Hypomagnesemia

  • > 2-4%: Confirms renal Mg wasting
  • Characteristic of: Gitelman syndrome, Bartter variants, diuretic use

Urine Calcium/Creatinine Ratio

Ratio = UCa (mg/dL) / UCr (mg/dL)
--
ratio

Bartter vs Gitelman Differentiation

  • < 0.02: Gitelman-like (hypocalciuria, thiazide effect)
  • > 0.20: Bartter-like (hypercalciuria, loop diuretic effect)

Nephron Segment Map

Proximal Tubule: FENa, FEUrea, FEPO4, TRP, TmP/GFR, FEUA (partial), glucose, amino acids

Thick Ascending Limb (TAL): FENa, FECl, FEK, FEMg, FECa (Bartter phenotype)

Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT): FENa, FECl, FEMg, FECa (Gitelman phenotype)

Cortical Collecting Duct: TTKG, UAG, acid excretion, K secretion
