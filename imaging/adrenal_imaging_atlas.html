💡 How to Use This Collection:
1. Radiopaedia cases offer interactive, annotated images — click through stacks to see CT/MRI slices.
2. PMC/Open Access articles are freely available full-text with figures and imaging.
3. Endotext chapters (NCBI Bookshelf) are complete, free textbook chapters with extensive references.
4. RadioGraphics/AJR articles are the gold-standard pictorial essays — some may require institutional access for full images.
5. The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA) provides downloadable DICOM datasets for research.
6. For adrenal washout calculations: APW >60% and RPW >40% = adenoma; Unenhanced CT ≤10 HU = lipid-rich adenoma.
🔬 Key Imaging Pearls for Endocrinology:
• Cushing Syndrome: Look for bilateral adrenal hyperplasia/nodularity, adenoma, or ACC. Use chemical shift MRI.
• Conn Syndrome (Primary Aldosteronism): Aldosteronoma (usually 1-2 cm), adrenal vein sampling for lateralization. Limb width >5 mm suggests hyperplasia.
• Pheochromocytoma: T2 "light bulb bright" (65% of cases), no signal loss on out-of-phase, avid enhancement. Rule out before biopsy!
• Adrenal Incidentaloma: ≤10 HU = benign. 11-20 HU + <4 cm = additional imaging. >20 HU or >4 cm = discuss MDT.
• ACC: Large (>6 cm), heterogeneous, necrosis, hemorrhage, calcification, venous invasion. IVC tumor thrombus on MRI.
